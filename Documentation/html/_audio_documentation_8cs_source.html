3  * @brief Everything related to playing audio.
4  * @{
5  * @section AudioInfo Information
6  * Samples for the piano and marimba were obtained from Electronic Music Studios at the University of Iowa (http://theremin.music.uiowa.edu/MIS.html).
7  * These samples are provided from Electronic Music Studios to the public for any use without restrictions.
8  * @n Samples for the drums were obtained from DrumGizmo (http://www.drumgizmo.org/wiki/doku.php?id=about)
9  * and are used under the Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported license (https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/).
10  * The kit used was the DRSKit (http://www.drumgizmo.org/wiki/doku.php?id=kits:drskit).
11  *
12  * @section Terms
13  *
14  * @subsection DefVel Velocity
15  * Velocity is a term taken from MIDI that refers to the volume of the note.
16  * Unlike MIDI where the velocity ranges from 0-127, the velocity of notes for
17  * this project range from 0-100 so that they can be used like a percentage.
18  * @n A velocity of 0 means that the note is silent. A velocity of 100
19  * means that the note is as loud as it can possibly be.
20  *
21  * @subsection DefBID Built-In Dynamics
22  * This term refers to the capability that some instruments have for using
23  * different sound files depending on the velocity of a note.
24  * @n Right now only the Piano supports this. There are three different sound
25  * files for each pitch which are labelled pp (pianissimo), mf (mezzoforte), or
26  * ff (fortissimo).
27  *
28  * @subsubsection DefBIDThresh Built-In Dynamics Thresholds
29  * This term refers to the thresholds for when to use a specific Built-In Dynamic.
30  * For example, the thresholds for the Piano are [50, 75, 100]. This means that velocities
31  * of 50 and under use the pp files, velocities of 51-75 use the mf files, and velocities of
32  * 76-100 use the ff files.
33  *
34  * @}

46  * @brief @copybrief VirtualInstrumentManager
47  * @{
48  * @section VIMIntro Introduction
49  * The VirtualInstrumentManager is a script for a Unity GameObject that manages a virtual instrument and its output.
50  * Its responsibilities include:
51  * @li Initialization of the audio code
52  * @li Serving as the bridge between non-audio objects and audio objects.
53  * @li Connecting each NoteOutputObject which handles the output of audio to the VirtualInstrument which holds the audio data.
54  * @li Mapping each Song to audio data from the VirtualInstrument and sending it to the proper NoteOutputObject.
55  * @li Sending parameters for audio effects to the AudioMixer provided by Unity.
56  * @li Handling Musical Typing Events.
57  * @li Passing data to ATI_Diagnostics.
